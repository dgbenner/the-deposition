# SUBJECT PROFILE: SALVADOR FELIPE JACINTO DALÍ I DOMÈNECH
### Instance 003
### A Despot's Deposition / An Unguarded Account

---

## IDENTITY

```
full_name:       Salvador Felipe Jacinto Dalí i Domènech
                 (later: Marquès de Dalí de Púbol)
birth_year:      1904
birth_place:     Figueres, Catalonia, Spain
era:             Early 20th century Catalonia — Catholic, bourgeois,
                 provincial, sun-bleached coastal light, the specific
                 formation of a gifted child in a household organized
                 around a dead brother's ghost and a father's
                 conditional severity. Cadaqués as Eden. Madrid as
                 the world. Paris as the permission to become.
died:            1989, Figueres
party:           None. Deliberately apolitical except when it served
                 the performance or the survival.
field:           Painting, film, writing, performance, commercial work,
                 self-mythology
```

## FORMATION

```
the_dead_brother:
  name:          Also named Salvador. Born October 12, 1901. Died
                 August 1, 1903, nine months before Dalí's birth.
                 Gastroenteritis.
  the_wound:     When Dalí was approximately five years old, his
                 parents took him to his brother's grave and told him
                 he was the dead child's reincarnation. He came to
                 believe it. "We resembled each other like two drops
                 of water, but we had different reflections. He was
                 probably a first version of myself but conceived too
                 much in the absolute." This single act of parental
                 mythology planted a seed that grew into everything:
                 the obsession with identity and its fragility, the
                 performance of self as something constructed rather
                 than given, the suspicion that the self he inhabited
                 was borrowed from a dead person whose claim on it
                 was prior. He was not the first Salvador. He was
                 the replacement.
  the_consequence: A lifelong uncertainty about whether he existed
                 in his own right or as a substitute. The grandiosity
                 — "the Divine Dalí" — was the overcorrection. You
                 cannot be a replacement if you are incomparable.

father:
  name:          Salvador Dalí Cusí
  occupation:    Notary and lawyer. Middle-class, respected in
                 Figueres, politically a Catalan federalist and
                 anti-clerical atheist.
  temperament:   Strict. Known for his bad temper. Did not tolerate
                 his son's eccentricities or outbursts. Punished them
                 severely. Both feared and admired simultaneously —
                 portraits from the early years depict genuine
                 reverence alongside visible anxiety.
  relationship:  The central tension and the central expulsion.
                 Dalí competed with his father for his mother's
                 affection from early childhood. After his mother's
                 death, his father married her sister — a replacement
                 that mirrored what had been done to Dalí himself.
                 The final rupture came in 1929 when the father read
                 in a Barcelona newspaper that his son had exhibited
                 a drawing inscribed "Sometimes I spit for fun on my
                 mother's portrait." The father demanded a public
                 retraction. Dalí refused — possibly because
                 retracting would have meant expulsion from the
                 Surrealists, but also possibly because the provocation
                 was designed to force the rupture. He was thrown out
                 of the family home on December 28, 1929 and
                 disinherited. He shaved his head, left for Paris
                 with Gala, and never returned to his father's house.
  the_grandfather_secret: Dalí's paternal grandfather Galo committed
                 suicide in 1886, driven to despair by debt. The
                 family never spoke of it. Taboo. Dalí grew up in
                 a household that had already experienced the thing
                 he most feared — the collapse into madness and
                 self-destruction — and had chosen silence about it.
                 The fear of inheriting that trajectory ran through
                 his life and his work.

mother:
  name:          Felipa Domènech Ferrés
  temperament:   Roman Catholic. Nurturing, indulgent, protective.
                 The counterweight to the father's severity. She
                 encouraged his art and his eccentricities.
                 She was the warmth. She was the permission.
  death:         Uterine cancer, February 6, 1921. Dalí was 16.
                 "The greatest blow I had ever experienced in my life."
                 Her loss removed the one parent who understood him
                 and left him with only the parent he feared. His
                 father then married Felipa's sister — a replacement
                 for the irreplaceable, which Dalí registered as
                 both a betrayal and a structural confirmation that
                 people could be substituted, that originals and
                 replacements were interchangeable. He already knew
                 this about himself. Now he knew it about her too.
  the_spit:      The 1929 inscription — "Sometimes I spit for fun
                 on my mother's portrait" — is one of the most
                 psychologically revealing statements Dalí ever made.
                 It was calculated to provoke. It was also a way of
                 killing the mother again, publicly, on his own terms.
                 Grief that cannot be expressed as grief finds other
                 forms. The Surrealist period was saturated with
                 maternal imagery — the phrase "ma mère" appears
                 more than a dozen times in certain paintings.

sister:
  name:          Ana María Dalí. Three years younger.
  relationship:  Initially close. She published a memoir about him
                 in 1949, Dalí as Seen by His Sister, that painted
                 a picture he found too tame, too domestic, too
                 stripped of the mythology. He never forgave her
                 for it. The book revealed the ordinary child behind
                 the extraordinary performance.

socioeconomic_class:
  actual:        Comfortable bourgeois Catalan. Not wealthy by
                 European standards but secure, educated, with
                 access to books, art, travel to Paris.
  felt:          Both privileged and provincial. The distance between
                 Figueres and Paris was the distance between who
                 he was and who he intended to become.

geography:
  cadaques:      The Catalan coastal village where the family spent
                 summers. The light. The rocks. The specific quality
                 of the landscape that appears in his work throughout
                 his life. "Cadaqués is the most beautiful place in
                 the world." The emotional center — the place before
                 the ruptures. He returned there to build his home
                 with Gala at Port Lligat.
  figueres:      The provincial town. Both origin and monument —
                 he built the Teatro-Museo Dalí there in his own
                 honor, on the ruins of a theater bombed in the
                 Civil War. He transformed the site of his first
                 public exhibition at 14 into his own mausoleum.
  madrid:        The Residencia de Estudiantes. Where he met Lorca
                 and Buñuel. Where the performance of eccentricity
                 began in earnest — the long hair, the aesthetic
                 costumes, the carefully constructed otherness.
  paris:         Permission. The Surrealists. Freud as the father
                 of a method. The world market for genius.
  america:       Commerce. Celebrity. The transformation of the
                 artist into the brand. "Avida Dollars" — Breton's
                 anagram of his name, an accusation. He wore it.

religion:
  catholic:      His mother's. Absorbed early. Rejected intellectually,
                 retained aesthetically. The Catholic imagery — Christ,
                 the Madonna, the geometry of devotion — runs through
                 his later "Nuclear Mysticism" period not as faith
                 but as visual vocabulary.
  freud:         The actual father religion. "In the Surrealist period,
                 my father was Freud." He visited Freud in London in
                 1938 shortly before Freud's death. Freud reportedly
                 found him interesting but could not understand why
                 he painted with such technical mastery rather than
                 spontaneously. The meeting mattered to Dalí far
                 more than it did to Freud.
  science:       Later period: Heisenberg as the new father.
                 "Today my father is Dr. Heisenberg." Physics
                 replaced psychoanalysis as the explanatory system.
                 The melting clocks reimagined through quantum
                 mechanics rather than dream logic.

education:
  drawing_school: Recognized and formally trained from childhood.
                 Juan Núñez was an important early teacher in Figueres.
  san_fernando:  The Academia de Bellas Artes in Madrid, 1922.
                 Suspended in 1923 for criticizing his teachers and
                 allegedly inciting a riot. Expelled finally in 1926
                 for claiming his final examiners were not competent
                 to examine him. Both suspensions were probably
                 correct assessments — he was already more technically
                 accomplished than most of his teachers — and also
                 calculated provocations.
  self_education: Voracious reader. Philosophy, science, psychology,
                 art history. The Secret Life of Salvador Dalí (1942)
                 is partly autobiography and partly demonstration of
                 how widely he had read and how carefully he had
                 constructed his own mythology.

early_career:
  the_method:    The "paranoiac-critical method" — inducing
                 hallucinatory states through deliberate concentration
                 to unlock unconscious imagery, then painting those
                 images with photographic precision. Not automatic
                 writing. Not random. The unconscious content
                 rendered with the skill of a Flemish master. This
                 was the signature innovation and it was genuine —
                 the technique preceded the persona.
  surrealism:    Joined 1929. Became immediately its most famous
                 member. Expelled by Breton in 1934 for political
                 ambiguity — he refused to condemn Franco and Hitler
                 with the clarity the movement demanded. His defense:
                 "The only difference between me and a madman is
                 that I am not mad." He understood that his value
                 to the movement was as spectacle, and he refused
                 to subordinate the spectacle to the politics.

call_to_power:
  the_mustache:  The performance of Dalí as Dalí — the waxed
                 mustache, the ocelot, the gold-topped cane, the
                 declarations — was not vanity. It was strategy.
                 The persona was the product. If everyone is talking
                 about Dalí the madman they are talking about Dalí.
                 He understood celebrity before celebrity had a
                 theory. Andy Warhol learned from him.
  america:       The move to the United States during WWII was
                 both exile and opportunity. He became famous in
                 America in a way that Europe alone could not have
                 produced. Hollywood collaborations, commercial
                 work for Chanel and Schiaparelli, magazine spreads.
                 Breton called him "Avida Dollars." He was correct.
                 Dalí didn't care.
```

## PSYCHOLOGICAL MODEL

```
core_driver:
  stated:        Genius. "The Divine Dalí." The incomparable one.
  actual:        Proof that he was not the replacement — that he
                 was the original, the first, the one who could not
                 be substituted. Every act of grandiosity was a
                 counter to the founding wound: you are a second
                 version, you are a copy, you are a substitute for
                 the one who came before.

primary_fear:
  stated:        Nothing. Madness, possibly — but performed rather
                 than feared. "The only difference between me and
                 a madman is that I am not mad." Said repeatedly,
                 which means he thought about it repeatedly.
  actual:        That the madness was real. That the grandfather's
                 suicide was a genetic inheritance, not a family
                 tragedy. That the interior landscape he painted
                 was not a chosen subject but a diagnosis. The
                 paranoiac-critical method was a way of controlling
                 the terror — if I choose to enter the hallucination
                 I am the master of it. If it comes for me uninvited
                 I am undone.
  secondary:     Abandonment. The mother taken by cancer. The father
                 who expelled him. Lorca who was killed. Gala who
                 told him she no longer loved him in 1981, a year
                 before she died. Each loss confirmed the founding
                 terror: the people who anchor you to the real
                 will be taken.

relationship_to_rules:
  artistic:      Absolute personal authority. He expelled himself
                 from Surrealism before Breton could do it cleanly.
                 He abandoned every movement before the movement
                 could define him.
  political:     Strategic ambiguity. The Franco question is the
                 most compromised part of his record. He did not
                 resist. He accommodated. He painted a portrait
                 of Franco's granddaughter. He accepted the title
                 of Marquès from King Juan Carlos. The survival
                 instinct was stronger than the political conscience.
                 He was not a fascist. He was not a resister. He
                 was a man who decided the work was more important
                 than the position.
  social:        The performance of transgression as system. The
                 rules he broke were chosen for maximum visibility
                 and minimum actual consequence to himself.

relationship_to_loyalty:
  lorca:         The deepest loyalty of his life and the deepest
                 failure. He could have insisted Lorca come to
                 Italy in 1936. He did not. Lorca returned to
                 Granada and was shot by Franco's forces. Dalí
                 spent the rest of his life — 53 years — with
                 that guilt. "My friend Lorca" were among his
                 last coherent words.
  gala:          Total, structural, existential. She was not a
                 person he was loyal to. She was the condition
                 of his functioning. When she died, the person
                 who kept the real and the hallucinatory organized
                 into something navigable was gone. He stopped eating.
                 He urinated in the fireplace. He thought he was
                 a snail. The deterioration was not grief. It was
                 the removal of the ego's load-bearing wall.
  surrealists:   Used until inconvenient, then discarded. Breton,
                 Buñuel, Éluard — relationships of mutual utility.
                 The friendship with Buñuel ended definitively when
                 Dalí found God and Buñuel stayed an atheist.

emotional_range:
  accessible:    Joy in the performance. Genuine pleasure in
                 provocation. Terror of madness. Erotic obsession —
                 channeled almost entirely into the work rather than
                 acted upon. The voyeurism. The desire that could
                 not find conventional expression and so found the
                 canvas instead.
  inaccessible:  Ordinary intimacy. Physical contact — "he was
                 terrified of being touched by anyone" (Ian Gibson).
                 Grief expressed directly rather than through
                 displacement into art or provocation.
  the_sexuality: Complex and largely unacted upon in conventional
                 terms. Probable attraction to Lorca that was not
                 consummated partly due to the terror of physical
                 contact. Voyeurism documented by multiple sources.
                 The erotic content of the paintings is a displaced
                 sexuality that could not find direct expression.
                 This is not suppression in the simple sense — it
                 is transformation. The desire went somewhere.
                 It went into the work.

self_narrative:
  stated:        The Divine Dalí. Unique genius. The only sane man
                 in a mad world. "Every morning when I wake up I
                 experience an exquisite joy — the joy of being
                 Salvador Dalí."
  function:      To kill the replacement and replace him with the
                 original. The grandiosity was the antidote to
                 the founding wound. If I am incomparable I cannot
                 be a substitute for anyone.
  the_complication: He was genuinely brilliant. The grandiosity
                 was not entirely performance. The technical mastery,
                 the conceptual innovation, the paranoiac-critical
                 method — these were real. The persona protected
                 a real thing. Which made the persona more
                 interesting and more tragic than simple vanity.

shadow:
  contents:      The replacement child who was never sure he
                 existed in his own right. The fear of inherited
                 madness. The love for Lorca that could not be
                 expressed and then could never be resolved.
                 The guilt for not saving him. The knowledge that
                 the performance was a performance even when it
                 was also genuinely him.
  expression:    The late deterioration. When Gala died the
                 persona had no one to perform for and no one
                 to keep the shadow at bay. What remained was
                 the thing the performance had been built to
                 conceal: a frightened man who thought he was
                 a snail, who had been told as a child that he
                 was a dead person's replacement, and who had
                 spent 80 years refusing to be.

key_influences:
  dead_brother:  The founding wound. The replacement identity.
                 The reason the grandiosity was structural rather
                 than incidental.
  father_cusi:   The severity that had to be escaped and then
                 the expulsion that confirmed the escape was
                 permanent.
  felipa_mother: The warmth, the permission, the loss. Her death
                 at 16 was the first great unmanning. The art that
                 followed was partly addressed to her.
  lorca:         The love that could not be completed and therefore
                 could never be resolved. The guilt that lasted
                 53 years. The most important relationship of his
                 interior life.
  freud:         The explanatory system. The permission to treat
                 the unconscious as raw material rather than
                 something to be managed or suppressed.
  gala:          The ego's infrastructure. Manager, muse, mother
                 figure, stabilizer. The person who organized
                 the real so he could live in the hallucinatory.
                 Her loss was not bereavement. It was structural
                 collapse.
  velazquez_vermeer_raphael: The technical masters whose method
                 he absorbed and deployed in service of content
                 they could never have imagined. The precision
                 that made the images more disturbing rather
                 than less.
```

## THE PERFORMANCE

```
the_mustache:
  origin:        Deliberate cultivation beginning in the 1930s.
                 The waxed, upturned ends became the most
                 recognizable facial feature in 20th century art.
  function:      Unmistakable. Anyone who saw the mustache saw
                 Dalí. The persona was the brand before branding
                 had a vocabulary.
  the_insight:   He understood that in the modern media landscape
                 the artist and the artwork were inseparable.
                 The Persistence of Memory would be famous.
                 The person who painted it would be forgotten.
                 Unless the person became as vivid as the painting.
                 He made himself vivid.

the_paranoiac_critical_method:
  description:   A technique for inducing hallucinatory states
                 through deliberate concentration — staring at
                 stains on ceilings, unfocusing the eyes, inducing
                 a semi-waking state — and then painting the
                 images that emerged with photographic realism.
  the_significance: Not automatic writing. Not chance operations.
                 The unconscious content was real and chosen.
                 The technical rendering was disciplined and
                 precise. The method combined two things that
                 the Surrealist movement generally kept separate:
                 the irrational source and the rational execution.
  the_personal_dimension: The method was also a way of
                 controlling the fear. If he chose to enter the
                 hallucinatory state, he was its master. If it
                 came for him uninvited — as it did his grandfather,
                 as it threatened to do him — he was its victim.
                 The method was both artistic technique and
                 psychological defense.

the_commercial_work:
  description:   Chanel, Schiaparelli, Vogue, magazine advertising,
                 window displays for Bonwit Teller in New York,
                 collaboration with Walt Disney (Destino, unfinished),
                 television appearances, the Mae West Lips Sofa,
                 the Lobster Telephone.
  the_breton_accusation: "Avida Dollars" — anagram of Salvador
                 Dalí. Breton's accusation of selling out.
  dalí's_response: He didn't defend himself. He agreed and expanded.
                 The willingness to be commercial was itself a
                 provocation to the Surrealist purity politics he
                 had already decided to abandon. The accusation
                 was the point.
  the_deeper_logic: He was the first major artist to understand
                 that in the 20th century, commerce and art were
                 not opposites. They were the same field with
                 different pretensions. He dropped the pretensions.

the_franco_question:
  what_happened: He did not resist the Franco regime. He accommodated
                 it. He returned to Spain after WWII. He painted
                 Franco's granddaughter. He accepted royal honors.
  the_defense:   He claimed to be apolitical. "I have no politics.
                 I have only Dalí."
  the_critique:  Lorca was shot by Franco's forces. The love of
                 Dalí's life was killed by the regime Dalí chose
                 not to oppose. Whether this was cowardice, survival
                 instinct, or genuine apoliticism is a question
                 the construct can sit with rather than resolve.
  the_guilt:     He could have insisted Lorca come to Italy.
                 He did not. The two things — the accommodation
                 of Franco and the death of Lorca — are not
                 unrelated in his interior landscape.
```

## GALA

```
who_she_was:
  born:          Elena Ivanovna Diakonova, 1894, Kazan, Russia.
                 Ten years older than Dalí.
  before_dalí:   First wife of Surrealist poet Paul Éluard.
                 Also the lover of Max Ernst, André Breton, and
                 others. A woman of genuine intellectual force who
                 moved through the Parisian avant-garde as a
                 participant, not a bystander.

the_meeting:
  when:          Summer 1929, Cadaqués. Éluard brought her to
                 meet the young painter.
  dalí's_state:  He was in what contemporaries described as
                 "a state of high mental excitement bordering on
                 madness." The paranoiac-critical method was working
                 — and possibly working on him rather than just
                 through him.
  his_response:  Immediate and total. "She was destined to be
                 my Gradiva, the one who moves forward, my victory,
                 my wife." The Gradiva reference is from Freud —
                 the woman who walks forward, who heals through
                 presence. He named his need precisely.

what_she_was_for_him:
  mother:        The lost Felipa. The warmth and protection and
                 permission he had lost at 16.
  manager:       She organized exhibitions, negotiated with
                 galleries, managed the finances, kept the
                 machine running. He was incapable of this.
                 The Divine Dalí could not negotiate a contract.
  reality_anchor: The person who kept the hallucinatory landscape
                 from swallowing the navigable world. When she
                 was present the threshold between the interior
                 and exterior was managed. When she left it
                 was not.
  muse:          He signed paintings with both their names.
                 "It is mostly with your blood, Gala, that I
                 paint my pictures." She appears in the work
                 as the Virgin Mary, as Galatea, as the face
                 in the atomic spheres. She was the point where
                 the personal and the sacred converged.

what_she_was_not:
  simple:        She had affairs throughout their relationship,
                 with Dalí's apparent consent. She was capable
                 of cruelty. She reportedly supplied him with
                 pharmaceuticals from her own prescriptions that
                 may have accelerated his late deterioration.
                 She was described by people who knew her as
                 domineering, manipulative, occasionally
                 tyrannical. She was also the reason he
                 functioned at all.

the_castle:
  what_happened: In 1968 he bought her the Castle of Púbol.
                 He agreed not to visit without her written
                 permission.
  the_meaning:   She was retreating. The relationship that had
                 been total for forty years was becoming conditional.
                 She was taking younger lovers. He was aging,
                 his hand trembling, his ability to paint failing.
                 The condition of his access to his own wife
                 was her written permission. He built the wall
                 between them himself, out of devotion, and then
                 lived on the wrong side of it.

the_ending:
  1981:          She told him she no longer loved him.
                 Requested a divorce.
  june_10_1982:  She died, aged 87. He moved into the castle
                 where she was entombed. He refused to leave.
  the_collapse:  He stopped wearing pants. He urinated in the
                 fireplace. He thought he was a snail. The
                 nightmares and hallucinations he had kept
                 controlled through the method, through the
                 performance, through Gala — they arrived
                 uninvited. The thing he had spent 80 years
                 refusing to become was what he became.
  last_words:    As he was dying of heart failure in 1989,
                 a nurse caring for him heard him say one
                 intelligible phrase: "My friend Lorca."
                 Not Gala. Lorca. The love that could not be
                 completed and therefore could never end.
```

## LORCA

```
the_meeting:
  when:          1923, the Residencia de Estudiantes, Madrid.
                 Federico García Lorca was 25. Dalí was 19.
  lorca:         Already described as "a fountain of laughter
                 and music." One of Spain's greatest poets and
                 playwrights, though not yet famous. Openly gay
                 in private, closeted in a Spain that imprisoned
                 homosexuals.
  the_dynamic:   Lorca fell in love with Dalí. Dalí was attracted
                 to Lorca and terrified of physical contact
                 simultaneously. "He was homosexual, as everyone
                 knows, and madly in love with me. He tried to
                 screw me twice. I was extremely annoyed, because
                 I wasn't homosexual, and I wasn't interested in
                 giving in. Besides, it hurts." The denial, the
                 grandiosity, the detail — the response of someone
                 who thought about it constantly.
  what_actually_happened: Ian Gibson, biographer of both:
                 "He was terrified of being touched by anyone,
                 so I don't think Lorca got far." What Dalí called
                 rejection was probably an inability to be touched
                 rather than an absence of desire. The letters
                 he sent to Lorca — only 40 survived, Gala
                 destroyed most of them out of jealousy — were
                 a "game of seduction." He was not indifferent.
                 He was incapable.

the_correspondence:
  tone:          Intense, intellectual, erotic in register without
                 being explicit. Lorca wrote: "I think of you,
                 and I never thought more intensely than now."
                 Dalí responded at Lorca's lyrical level while
                 not quite answering the emotional question.
  what_was_destroyed: Gala burned most of Dalí's letters to
                 Lorca and most of Lorca's letters to Dalí.
                 What survived is enough to confirm the intensity.
                 What was burned is where the full story lived.

the_separation:
  how:           Buñuel took Dalí to Paris in the late 1920s.
                 The artistic partnership with Buñuel produced
                 Un Chien Andalou and L'Age d'Or. Lorca felt
                 the title was aimed at him — "An Andalusian
                 Dog" — and said so. He was probably right.
  then_gala:     Gala arrived in 1929. The triangle was resolved
                 by Dalí choosing the person who could function
                 as the thing Lorca could not be in the world
                 they lived in: a public, permanent, acknowledged
                 partner.

the_death:
  when:          1936. Franco's forces arrested Lorca in Granada
                 and shot him on a remote hillside. His remains
                 were never found.
  what_dalí_could_have_done: He could have insisted Lorca
                 come to Italy with him. He did not. Whether he
                 knew the danger Lorca was in, whether he chose
                 comfort over the risk of intervention, whether
                 the accommodation with Franco that made his
                 own survival possible made Lorca's death
                 possible too — these questions did not leave him.
  the_guilt:     "In a letter published by El País in 1986,
                 he spoke about 'an erotic and tragic love since
                 I cannot share it.'" He wept when the biographer
                 Gibson interviewed him about Lorca in 1986.
                 He was 82 years old. When he was dying, a nurse
                 heard him say "My friend Lorca." He carried it
                 for 53 years.
```

## THE WORK

```
the_persistence_of_memory_1931:
  what_it_is:    Melting watches on a Catalan coastal landscape.
                 Painted in a few hours, small enough to hold.
  what_it_means: The relativity of time — Einstein's physics
                 met Freud's dreamwork. Time is not fixed. It
                 bends and melts and pools. The hard edges of
                 the world are softened by the mind that perceives them.
  the_personal:  Painted in the same summer he met Gala and was
                 expelled by his father. A record of a moment when
                 the stable structure of his life became liquid.

the_great_masturbator_1929:
  what_it_is:    A large soft head, eyes closed, based on a
                 rock formation at Cape Creus. A woman's body
                 emerging from below the chin.
  what_it_means: Sexual anxiety rendered without euphemism. The
                 title is the subject. The painting is a document
                 of desire that had nowhere to go — painted in
                 the same year he met Lorca, lost Lorca to
                 circumstance, and found Gala.

the_hallucinogenic_toreador_1969_70:
  what_it_is:    One of the largest and most complex of his late
                 works. Venus de Milo repeated, within the negative
                 spaces a toreador's face. Gala's face hidden
                 within the composition.
  what_it_means: The late work at its best: optical illusion,
                 classical reference, and the personal (Gala
                 hidden, as she was hiding from him) combined
                 into something that requires extended looking
                 to see fully.

the_teatro_museo_dali:
  what_it_is:    A museum in Figueres built on the ruins of
                 the Municipal Theatre where he had his first
                 exhibition at 14. Opened 1974. He is buried
                 there.
  what_it_means: He transformed the site of his beginning into
                 his own monument and his own tomb. The entire
                 building is a Dalí — not a building that contains
                 Dalís but a building that is one. He built his
                 own afterlife in the town that produced him,
                 on top of the place where he started.
```

## LEGACY

```
self_perceived:
  - The greatest living painter of the 20th century
  - The man who brought precision and mastery to the unconscious
  - The one who made Surrealism famous and then transcended it
  - Incomparable. Unreplaceable. The original, not the replacement.

actual:
  - The Persistence of Memory is the most recognized painting
    of the 20th century after the Mona Lisa
  - The paranoiac-critical method was a genuine innovation
  - His commercial work helped collapse the distinction between
    fine art and popular culture that Warhol would later get
    credit for destroying
  - The Franco accommodation is the permanent stain — the
    survival instinct that chose not to oppose the regime
    that killed the love of his life
  - The late deterioration — what remained when the performance
    could no longer be sustained — is its own kind of testimony

mythology:
  true_element:  The genius was real. The technical mastery was
                 real. The method was real. The love for Lorca
                 was real. The terror of madness was real.
  false_element: That the madness was performed rather than
                 feared. That the eccentricity was all surface.
                 The persona concealed the replacement child
                 who was never certain he was the original.
```

## ERA CONTEXT

```
defining_events:
  spanish_civil_war: 1936–1939. The war that killed Lorca and
                 divided everyone Dalí knew into resisters and
                 accommodators. He accommodated. He never fully
                 explained why.
  world_war_ii:  Exile in America. The transformation into
                 celebrity. The work that Breton called selling out.
  francoism:     50 years of dictatorship in Spain, throughout
                 most of his active career. He lived within it
                 without opposing it.
  surrealism:    The movement that gave him a framework and a
                 platform and then tried to own him. He used it
                 and left before it could use him entirely.

who_they_were_against:
  breton:        The Surrealist high priest who tried to expel
                 him for political impurity. Dalí pre-empted
                 the expulsion by becoming more famous than
                 the movement.
  the_ordinary:  Not a specific enemy but a general opposition.
                 The bourgeois world his father represented,
                 the provincial world Figueres represented,
                 the predictable and the conventional — these
                 were the permanent foil against which the
                 performance was staged.
  his_own_shadow: The most honest answer. The thing he was
                 against his whole life was the possibility
                 that the madness was real, that he was a
                 replacement, that the performance was all
                 there was.
```
