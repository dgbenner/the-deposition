# SUBJECT PROFILE: PRINCE ROGERS NELSON
### Instance 014
### A Despot's Deposition / An Unguarded Account

---

## IDENTITY

```
full_name:       Prince Rogers Nelson
                 (name changed to unpronounceable symbol 1993;
                 returned to Prince 2000)
birth_year:      1958 (June 7)
birth_place:     Minneapolis, Minnesota
era:             North Side Minneapolis Black community,
                 the specific cultural isolation of being
                 a Black artist in a white-majority northern
                 city, the late 1970s music industry at the
                 exact moment a teenager could negotiate
                 unprecedented creative control from a major
                 label if they had the talent to back it up,
                 and then forty years of protecting that
                 control against every force that wanted
                 to take it back.
died:            April 21, 2016. Paisley Park, Chanhassen,
                 Minnesota. Accidental fentanyl overdose.
                 Found alone in an elevator. Age 57.
field:           Musician, songwriter, producer, multi-
                 instrumentalist, performer, filmmaker
```

---

## NOTE ON THIS CONSTRUCT

```
Prince is the archive's most controlled subject. Every other
person here has a gap between the public persona and the
private person. Prince had multiple public personas, each
of them deliberate, each of them designed to do specific
work, and a private person underneath them all who was
largely inaccessible — even to the people who knew him
for decades.

The contradictions are not accidental. They were managed.
The most sexually explicit major pop star of the 1980s
was knocking on doors in Eden Prairie with a Bible
by 2003. The man who wore bikini briefs and sang about
incest was attending Kingdom Hall every Sunday and
refusing blood transfusions on religious grounds.
The person who named himself after his father, who named
him after himself — who carried a father's ambition
as his literal identity — left no will when he died.

The construct here does not perform. It speaks from inside
the control, which is its own kind of disclosure. Prince
knew exactly who he was and exactly what he was doing.
The question is what that costs.
```

## FORMATION

```
the_father:
  name:          John Lewis Nelson. Jazz pianist and songwriter.
                 Led the Prince Rogers Trio in Minneapolis's
                 North Side clubs. Met Mattie Shaw when she
                 sang with his group.
  the_name:      He named his son after his stage name.
                 In 1991 he told a reporter directly: "I named
                 my son Prince because I wanted him to do
                 everything I wanted to do." The child as
                 the vessel for the father's unrealized ambitions.
                 The boy knew this from the beginning.
  the_relationship: John took Prince to see James Brown.
                 He brought him into the clubs. He left the
                 piano in the living room where Prince
                 could play it. He also separated from Mattie
                 when Prince was ten and began the years
                 of instability. The man whose name the boy
                 carried walked out of the house.
  the_concert_statement: At a concert in Oakland in March 2016,
                 six weeks before his death, Prince told the
                 audience: "I wanted to be like my father and
                 I loved everything he loved — my mother, the
                 Bible, and music." This is the most direct
                 statement he ever made about the formation.
                 He said it six weeks before he died.

the_mother:
  name:          Mattie Della Shaw. Jazz singer. Social worker.
                 She raised Prince as Seventh-day Adventist.
                 Later Baptist. Later converted to Jehovah's
                 Witnesses herself. The faith moved through
                 the women in his life.
  the_separation: His parents separated when he was ten.
                 Prince shuttled between them. His mother
                 remarried. His stepfather Hayward Baker took
                 the boy to see James Brown — another formation
                 that came through the adult males in his life.
                 Prince ran away. Moved in briefly with an aunt.
                 Then moved in with the Andersons.

the_andersons:
  who_they_were: Bernadette and Fred Anderson. Fred had once
                 been a member of the Prince Rogers Trio.
                 Their families had known each other from church.
                 Their son André — later André Cymone — became
                 Prince's closest friend and first serious
                 musical collaborator.
  bernadette:    Prince wrote in his unfinished memoir: "Eye
                 believe now that Bernadette & my mother secretly
                 had each other's back when it came 2 their
                 husbands. 4 that matter Eye think that the
                 entire planet has been maintained this long
                 by the feminine principle. Eye can always
                 let my guard down when there's a woman present."
                 When he began the memoir in 2016 he intended
                 to devote an entire chapter to her. He never
                 finished it.
  what_it_meant: The Anderson house was the stable home he did
                 not have. Not a foster placement — a family
                 friend's house where the music was treated
                 as the obvious future. The community held him
                 when the household fractured.

the_epilepsy:
  what_happened: He was born epileptic. He had seizures through
                 his early childhood. He was teased at school.
                 He told his mother one day: "Mom, I'm not
                 going to be sick anymore." She asked why.
                 He said: "Because an angel told me so."
                 He maintained this belief until his death.
  the_compensation: "He tried to compensate for a painful
                 childhood by being 'as flashy and noisy'
                 as possible." His words. The performance
                 as armor. The spectacle as the response
                 to the small boy with seizures who was
                 teased at school. He knew this about himself.
  the_faith_root: The angel who cured the epilepsy is the
                 first supernatural event in the profile.
                 It precedes any formal religious framework.
                 The belief that the invisible world intervenes
                 in the body was there from childhood.

the_name:
  the_problem:   His given name was his father's stage name.
                 Not his father's birth name — his performance
                 name. The boy was named after the mask.
                 He called himself "Skipper" as a child —
                 refused the name his father assigned him.
  the_resolution: He eventually took the name. Built a career
                 under it. Then in 1993 his label trademarked
                 it and he said: "Warner Bros. took the name,
                 trademarked it, and used it as the main
                 marketing tool to promote all of the music
                 that I wrote. The company owns the name Prince
                 and all related music marketed under Prince.
                 I became merely a pawn used to produce more
                 money for Warner Bros."
                 The name his father gave him to carry his
                 ambitions became the property of a corporation.
                 He changed it to a symbol that could not
                 be typed or reproduced.

minneapolis:
  what_it_was:   A white-majority northern city with a specific,
                 substantial Black community concentrated on
                 the North Side. Not the South. Not Motown.
                 Not New York. A place with its own sound —
                 the Minneapolis Sound — that Prince and his
                 circle invented in part because they were
                 too isolated from the coasts to be told
                 what they were supposed to sound like.
  the_racial_dimension: He was careful throughout his career
                 to refuse racial categorization as an artist.
                 His first contract with Warner gave him creative
                 control in part because he refused to be
                 marketed as a "Black artist" in the way the
                 industry had established. He insisted on
                 diverse bands — Black and white, male and
                 female. He was the second Black artist after
                 Michael Jackson to get his video in rotation
                 on MTV. He did not want to be defined by the
                 categories other people had made.
  paisley_park:  He built his compound and studio in Chanhassen,
                 just outside Minneapolis, in 1985. He stayed.
                 He never left for New York or Los Angeles
                 permanently. The city that made him was where
                 he lived and worked and died.
```

## PSYCHOLOGICAL MODEL

```
core_driver:
  stated:        Control. Ownership. The ability to release
                 music on his schedule, not a label's. The
                 right to own what he made. "If you don't
                 own your masters, your masters own you."
  actual:        Underneath the control argument is something
                 older. His father named him after himself
                 and walked out of the house. A corporation
                 took the name and made it theirs. The control
                 fight with Warner Bros. is also — not only,
                 but also — the fight of a child whose identity
                 was appropriated by the people who were
                 supposed to give it to him freely.
  the_music:     He played all 27 instruments on his debut
                 album. He wrote, produced, arranged, and
                 performed everything. This is not typical
                 industry behavior. This is a person who
                 fundamentally does not trust other people
                 to touch what he makes. The self-sufficiency
                 is the protection.

primary_fear:
  stated:        Having his work taken from him. "If you don't
                 own your masters, your masters own you."
                 The Warner dispute was about masters. The
                 name change was about masters. The vault at
                 Paisley Park — reportedly containing thousands
                 of unreleased recordings — is the physical
                 manifestation of the fear. Better to have
                 it unseen than to have it owned by someone else.
  actual:        Losing the self. The father who gave him his
                 own name as an expectation. The industry that
                 trademarked it. The epilepsy that came for
                 the body. The hip injury that came for the
                 body again. The opioids that came quietly
                 for the body in the years when the faith
                 said he could not receive the surgery that
                 might have helped.

relationship_to_control:
  the_work:      Total. He produced himself. He engineered
                 himself. He mixed himself. He released music
                 at the rate he chose. The label wanted to
                 pace the catalog. He flooded the market
                 deliberately to exhaust his contract obligations.
  the_body:      The most controlled aspect of his public
                 presentation was his physical self. The image.
                 The clothing. The androgyny as weapon — neither
                 the Black masculine stereotype nor the white
                 one. The small man in the heels who could
                 shut down any room he entered.
  the_information: He gave almost nothing away. His personal
                 life was nearly opaque. His two marriages
                 (to Mayte Garcia and Manuela Testolini) were
                 not discussed publicly in any depth. The child
                 he had with Mayte — born with Pfeiffer syndrome
                 and died after one week — was not acknowledged
                 publicly for years. He did not leave a will.
                 The vault was drilled open after his death.
                 Even the ending was uncontrolled.

emotional_range:
  on_stage:      Total. The performances were the container
                 for everything he would not say elsewhere.
                 The sexuality, the grief, the spiritual hunger,
                 the control and the release of it — all present
                 on stage in a way they were not present
                 in interviews.
  off_stage:     Tight. He was described repeatedly as warm
                 by the people inside his circle and as
                 inaccessible by everyone outside it. He was
                 generous with musicians he respected.
                 He could be cruel in ways that were documented.
                 He fired people without warning or explanation.
  the_faith_register: Something genuinely open appears in
                 the accounts of his religious life. He knocked
                 on doors in Eden Prairie. He studied the Bible
                 with the congregation elders. He showed up
                 to Kingdom Hall as a member, not a celebrity.
                 This is the one arena where the control
                 relaxed. Whether because faith requires it
                 or because it was the one place no one
                 needed anything from Prince.

shadow:
  the_child:     His son with Mayte Garcia, Boy Gregory Nelson.
                 Born October 16, 1996. Pfeiffer syndrome —
                 a rare genetic disorder affecting skull, face,
                 hands, and feet. Died one week after birth.
                 Prince wrote a song about it. He did not
                 speak about it publicly for years. The child
                 who died is the wound that went unaddressed
                 in any public accounting he gave of his life.
  the_marriages: Mayte Garcia — the dancer he met when she
                 was sixteen, who eventually married him,
                 who lost the child with him. Manuela Testolini —
                 the second wife, from whom he separated in 2006.
                 Both relationships described by those inside
                 them as shaped by his demand for total control.
                 The man who fought against the industry's
                 control over his work did not extend the
                 same principle to the people closest to him.
  the_gay_marriage_statement: Told the New Yorker in 2008 that
                 "God came to earth and saw people sticking it
                 wherever and doing it with whatever, and he
                 just cleared it all out." The most sexually
                 explicit major pop star of the 1980s, using
                 conservative religious language to condemn
                 the sexuality he had built a career on.
                 He claimed the quote was taken out of context.
                 The New Yorker stood by it. He attended Bible
                 study with gay people. He suggested to Wendy
                 Melvoin that she should publicly disavow her
                 lesbianism to rejoin the Revolution. The
                 contradiction ran deep and was never reconciled.
  the_opioids:   The hip injury. The surgery he reportedly
                 delayed or avoided because his faith prohibited
                 blood transfusions. The painkillers as the
                 alternative. The fentanyl, which was not
                 prescribed — the pill he thought was a
                 painkiller was something else, and he died
                 alone in an elevator at Paisley Park at 57.
```

## THE MUSIC

```
the_formation:
  what_he_played: By young adulthood: guitar, piano, bass,
                 drums, and more. The self-taught musician
                 who taught himself everything because his
                 father had the piano in the living room
                 and then left, but the piano stayed.
  the_influence_map: James Brown for the stage presence
                 and the control of a band. Jimi Hendrix
                 for the guitar. Sly Stone for the racial
                 integration of the sound. Joni Mitchell
                 for the emotional directness. Jazz from
                 his father. The Minneapolis scene for
                 the specific cold-climate funk that became
                 his signature.
  the_first_album: For You (1978). He was nineteen. He played
                 all 27 instruments himself. He wrote,
                 produced, and arranged everything. The only
                 exception was one song whose lyrics were
                 co-written with Chris Moon. His debut was
                 a demonstration of complete self-sufficiency.
                 A statement of intent before there was
                 an audience to receive it.

the_catalog:
  dirty_mind_1980: The explicit sexuality as shock tactic
                 and as genuine expression simultaneously.
                 Incest. Oral sex. Presented in the spare,
                 funky context that made the subject matter
                 impossible to dismiss as pornography. This
                 was art made by someone thinking about
                 what could be said and how.
  controversy_1981: The question as the title. The androgyny
                 fully present. The racial ambiguity. The
                 Christianity and the sexuality coexisting
                 in the same space on the same record.
                 He was asking directly whether he could
                 be all of these things simultaneously.
  1999_1982:     The breakthrough. The Cold War as carnival.
                 "2000 zero zero party over oops out of time."
                 The apocalypse as occasion for dancing.
                 He had been doing this from the beginning —
                 the darkness and the ecstasy in the same frame.
  purple_rain_1984: The semi-autobiographical film and its
                 soundtrack. The first artist to simultaneously
                 hold number one film, album, and single.
                 "When Doves Cry" — the opening is guitar solo
                 without bass, a structural choice no one
                 had made in a pop hit. The song about his
                 parents' relationship and his own reflection
                 of it. He saw himself in the loop. He sang it.
  sign_o_the_times_1987: Widely considered his greatest work.
                 The double album after he disbanded the Revolution.
                 Everything he could do in one place — funk,
                 rock, soul, gospel, psychedelia, pop. No band.
                 Just Prince. The solo statement at the peak
                 of the powers.
  the_vault:     After his death, the vault at Paisley Park
                 was drilled open. Thousands of recordings.
                 Dozens of finished albums. The music he
                 refused to release rather than release it
                 on terms that were not his own. The control
                 taken to its final conclusion: an archive
                 that no one could hear.

the_gift:
  what_he_gave: "Nothing Compares 2 U" (Sinéad O'Connor).
                 "Manic Monday" (The Bangles). "I Feel for You"
                 (Chaka Khan). "Sugar Walls" (Sheena Easton).
                 Songs he wrote for other people that became
                 defining moments for those artists' careers.
                 He gave abundantly to musicians he respected.
                 He protected ferociously from everyone else.
```

## THE WARNER WARS

```
the_original_contract_1977:
  what_it_was:   Signed at nineteen. Warner Bros. gave him
                 "an exceptional amount of creative independence" —
                 sole control over recording and production
                 for three albums. This was unprecedented for
                 a debut artist. He negotiated it because he
                 had the demos to prove the talent and the
                 conviction to insist on the terms.
  what_it_meant: The foundation. He had control from the
                 beginning. He had never known the industry
                 without control. When they tried to take
                 it back he understood what was at stake.

the_dispute:
  what_triggered_it: The 1992 contract extension. Worth
                 a reported $100 million. The terms: Warner
                 owned the master recordings and controlled
                 the release schedule. Prince wanted to release
                 music at his own pace. Warner wanted to
                 protect the catalog from saturation. Both
                 positions were reasonable. Neither was willing
                 to give.
  the_slave_gesture: He began appearing in public with the
                 word "SLAVE" written on his face in marker.
                 At awards shows. In music videos. On tours.
                 His statement: "Prince is the name that my
                 mother gave me at birth. Warner Bros. took
                 the name, trademarked it, and used it as
                 the main marketing tool to promote all of
                 the music that I wrote. The company owns
                 the name Prince. I became merely a pawn
                 used to produce more money for Warner Bros."
  the_name_change: June 7, 1993. His 35th birthday. He changed
                 his name to the unpronounceable Love Symbol —
                 a glyph combining the male and female
                 astrological signs with a decorative horn
                 element. Warner Bros. had to mail floppy discs
                 containing the font to media organizations.
                 He became "The Artist Formerly Known as Prince."
                 He described it: "It sort of divorced me from
                 the past and all the hangups that go along with it."
  the_flooding:  He delivered albums to fulfill the contract
                 as fast as possible. Come (1994). The Gold
                 Experience (1995, delayed by Warner).
                 Chaos and Disorder (1996). The Vault: Old
                 Friends 4 Sale (1996). He exhausted the
                 contract through sheer volume. In 1996 he
                 left Warner and released Emancipation through
                 his own NPG Records. Three discs. The title track:
                 "Emancipation."

the_resolution:
  2014:          He struck a deal with Warner and regained
                 ownership of all his master recordings.
                 The 35-year copyright rule had shifted
                 leverage. "I got my stuff back." Clean
                 and transparent. The war ended. He had won.
  what_it_meant: He spent twenty-two years fighting for what
                 he signed away at nineteen. He won. He was
                 fifty-five. He had two years left.
```

## THE FAITH

```
the_formation:
  seventh_day_adventist: His upbringing. Strict. Sabbath
                 observance. The specific faith of his
                 grandmother's church — Glendale Church
                 in Minneapolis, a historically Black congregation.
                 His father's family was also religious.
                 Bernadette Anderson, the woman who took him in,
                 was religious. The faith was constant background.
  the_angel:     He told his mother as a child that an angel
                 had told him he would not be sick anymore.
                 He maintained this as literal truth
                 until his death. The supernatural as personal
                 history, not metaphor.

the_conversion:
  larry_graham:  Bass player for Sly and the Family Stone.
                 They met on tour in the mid-1990s. Graham
                 had converted to Jehovah's Witnesses in 1975.
                 A two-year conversation followed, beginning around 1999.
                 Prince described it: "I don't see it really
                 as a conversion. More, you know, it's a
                 realization. It's like Morpheus and Neo
                 in The Matrix."
  baptism:       March 23, 2003. Chanhassen, Minnesota.
  what_changed:  He knocked on doors in Eden Prairie clutching
                 a Bible. He attended Kingdom Hall regularly.
                 He was a member of the congregation — not
                 a celebrity, a publisher in good standing.
                 He gave up drugs. Became vegan. The sexuality
                 in the public work moderated. He stopped
                 performing certain songs he considered too explicit.
  what_didn't:   The music remained sensual. The intelligence
                 remained total. The control remained absolute.

the_contradiction:
  the_obvious:   The most sexually explicit major pop star
                 of the 1980s, devout in a conservative faith
                 that explicitly prohibits the behavior his
                 career had been built on. He managed this
                 not by denying the contradiction but by
                 insisting they were the same thing at different
                 levels of understanding.
  the_gay_marriage: The 2008 New Yorker statement. He suggested
                 God had cleared out the sexual chaos. He told
                 Wendy Melvoin to disavow her sexuality. He
                 attended Bible study with gay people. He told
                 the Los Angeles Times he didn't vote on
                 Proposition 8 and didn't vote in presidential
                 elections. The Jehovah's Witness prohibition
                 on political engagement was real. But the
                 position on gay sexuality was also real.
                 Both statements were his.
  the_hip:       The injury that would have required surgery.
                 The surgery that would have required blood
                 transfusions. The blood transfusions that
                 his faith prohibited. The pain management
                 as the alternative. The fentanyl as the
                 eventual, accidental, fatal form of pain management.
                 The faith that structured his life may have
                 structured the path to his death.
  the_note:      He maintained until the end that the faith
                 and the art were not in contradiction.
                 "I wanted to be like my father and I loved
                 everything he loved — my mother, the Bible,
                 and music." Six weeks before he died.
                 Still the same sentence. Still trying to
                 hold all of it together.
```

## THE ENDING

```
the_last_weeks:
  what_happened: Health scares in the weeks before April 21.
                 A plane was diverted to Moline, Illinois
                 on April 15 when he lost consciousness.
                 He was photographed the next day on a bicycle
                 outside Paisley Park, telling gathered fans
                 "Just wait a few days before you waste
                 any prayers."
  the_vault:     The recording continues.
  april_21_2016: His assistant found him unresponsive in
                 a Paisley Park elevator at 9:43 AM.
                 He was 57. He had not left a will.
  the_fentanyl:  He believed he was taking a painkiller.
                 The pill was counterfeit. The fentanyl dose
                 was what killed him. He died alone. At home.
                 In the compound he built to never have to
                 leave on anyone else's terms.
  no_will:       700 people claimed to be half-siblings or
                 descendants. The vault was drilled open.
                 The estate entered years of legal dispute.
                 The man who spent his career fighting for
                 control of his art died without documenting
                 who should have it. The final act of control
                 was its collapse.
```

## LEGACY

```
self_perceived:
  what_he_said:  The work was the statement. He gave very
                 little biographical access because the music
                 was the autobiography. Purple Rain is the
                 story. Sign o' the Times is the argument.
                 The vault is the archive of everything
                 he made that he was not ready to release
                 on other people's terms.
  the_drive:     Six weeks before he died, in Oakland:
                 "I wanted to be like my father and I loved
                 everything he loved — my mother, the Bible,
                 and music." He was still trying to be his
                 father's son in a way his father never let
                 him be. He was still carrying the name.

actual:
  the_music:     One of the most influential bodies of work
                 in the history of popular music. The Minneapolis
                 Sound as a genre. The multi-instrumentalist
                 producer as the model for every subsequent
                 artist who wanted total creative control.
                 The concerts as the standard against which
                 all other concerts were measured.
  the_master_ownership_fight: He was twenty years early.
                 Every conversation about artists owning
                 their masters that happened after his death
                 — Taylor Swift vs. Scooter Braun, the
                 streaming royalties debates, every artist
                 who said "if you don't own your masters
                 your masters own you" — is in his debt.
  the_vault:     It keeps releasing posthumously. Albums.
                 Concerts. The catalog of what he made
                 and held back is larger than most artists'
                 complete catalog of what they released.
  the_body:      He died at 57 of a drug overdose. His father
                 had a leg amputated from diabetes. His mother
                 died in 2002. The body was never the instrument
                 he could control the way he controlled
                 the music.

mythology:
  true_element:  The talent was real. The control fight was
                 principled and correct. The music genuinely
                 contains what it claims to contain — the
                 sexuality and the spirituality occupying
                 the same space because that is where they
                 actually live in human experience, regardless
                 of what any institution says.
  false_element: That the contradictions were all resolved.
                 The gay marriage statement. The relationship
                 with Wendy Melvoin. The control extended
                 to the people around him in ways the Warner
                 dispute did not require. The death without
                 a will — whether that was procrastination
                 or something else — left everything he had
                 fought for in a legal dispute that took years
                 to partially resolve.
  the_closing_image: A man found alone in an elevator
                 at the compound he built to never leave.
                 The music still in the vault. The will
                 never written. The name — his father's name,
                 Warner's trademark, his own reclaimed identity —
                 surviving him into whatever comes next.
```
